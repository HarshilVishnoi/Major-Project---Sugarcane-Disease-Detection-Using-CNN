Know About Sugarcane Diseases

More than 50 diseases are reported in sugarcane. Fungi, bacteria, viruses and nematodes cause the most destructive diseases. These diseases affect different parts of the plant and may occur in any field under favorable conditions.

Early identification of sugarcane diseases plays a crucial role in preventing crop loss and improving overall yield. Traditional disease identification methods are time-consuming and require expert knowledge. By integrating modern computer vision and deep learning techniques, farmers can detect diseases at an early stage and take timely preventive measures.

This platform aims to assist farmers, researchers, and agricultural experts by providing an intelligent system that analyzes sugarcane leaf images and predicts the disease category with high accuracy, along with basic treatment recommendations.

The deep learning model focuses on important visual regions of the leaf, such as discoloration, texture variations, and lesion patterns. During training, the CNN learns hierarchical features starting from edges and colors to complex disease-specific patterns. The final prediction is based on the region that contributes the most to disease classification.

— DISEASES —

Banded Chlorosis

Alternating yellow and green bands appear along the length of the leaves. Gives the leaf a distinct striped look and reduces photosynthesis.

➜

Brown Spot

Small brown or dark lesions develop on leaf surfaces. Spots may merge, causing leaf drying and reduced plant vigor.

➜

Brown Rust

Reddish-brown rust-like pustules appear mainly on leaves. Severe infection leads to premature leaf drying and yield loss.

➜

Dried Leaves

Leaves lose moisture and turn dry from tip to base. Often caused by stress, nutrient deficiency, or disease infection.

➜

Grassy Shoot

Multiple thin, grassy shoots emerge from the base of the plant. Affected plants show stunted growth and do not produce cane.

➜

Top Rot

The top portion of the cane shows rotting and soft tissue decay. Leaves near the top wilt, yellow, and eventually die.

➜

Pokkah Boeng

Young leaves show twisting, distortion, and reddish streaks. Severe cases cause top rot and malformed cane growth.

➜

Sett Rot

Seed setts decay with soft, dark rotting tissues. Results in poor germination and uneven crop establishment.

➜

Smut

Black whip-like structures emerge from the shoot tip. Plants show reduced growth and fail to develop healthy canes.

➜

Viral Disease

Mosaic patterns, streaks, or uneven coloration appear on leaves. Growth slows down and overall plant health declines.

➜

Yellow Leaf

Leaves turn yellow starting from the midrib outward. Sugar accumulation and cane yield are significantly reduced.

➜

Red Rot

The internal tissue of the cane turns red with white patches. Causes severe yield loss and can lead to complete crop failure.
